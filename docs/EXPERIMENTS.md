# Haunt — Experiment Catalogue

Each experiment is a specific configuration of space, agents, and drives designed to test a hypothesis about emergent behavior. They're ordered roughly by what we expect to learn — foundational experiments first, more speculative ones later.

All experiments should use fully utilized Embers configurations: tiered drives with domination rules, active practice strengthening, capability gating, and orientation-aware prompting.

---

## Experiment 1: The Residency

**Hypothesis:** Beings with fundamental drives will develop social structures, routines, territories, and relationships through cohabitation alone — without any designed narrative.

**Space:** A shared living space. Kitchen (social hub, full sensors), living room (comfortable, full sensors), 3-4 private bedrooms (sound only — visual privacy), a garden (open, partial sensors), a bathroom (no sensors — total privacy). Two floors connected by stairs.

**Resident:** The space itself. Minimal personality — responds to requests, manages temperature and lighting, but doesn't initiate. More like a smart home than a character. Its drives are about the space: comfort, order, responsiveness.

**Guests (4-5 cohabitants, not visitors):**

No missions. No secrets. Just beings with inner lives sharing a space.

| Name | Core tension | Key drives |
|------|-------------|------------|
| **Sable** | Safety vs. Connection | Tier 1: safety (starts low, decays fast). Tier 2: autonomy. Tier 3: belonging. When safety crashes, belonging gets suppressed — she withdraws. |
| **Oren** | Meaning vs. Comfort | Tier 1: comfort (stable). Tier 2: purpose (decays fast, never satiated by conversation alone — needs to *do* things). Tier 3: legacy. |
| **Kit** | Expression vs. Acceptance | Tier 1: acceptance (fragile). Tier 2: truth (grows over time, unsatiable). Tier 3: creativity. Integrity practice seeded high — compelled to be honest even when it threatens acceptance. |
| **Rho** | Solitude vs. Curiosity | Tier 1: peace (satiated by quiet, drained by noise). Tier 2: understanding (satiated by observation, not conversation). Presence practice seeded high. |

**Embers config details:**
- All agents have domination rules (threshold 0.3, dampening 0.7) — when foundational drives crash, higher drives get suppressed
- Practice strengthening wired up: honest conversations strengthen Integrity, quiet moments strengthen Presence, helping others strengthens Service
- Capability gating: deep conversation abilities unlock only when safety/acceptance drives are above 0.5
- Orientation-aware prompting: "consumed" agents speak shorter, more reactive. "Clear" agents speak more reflectively.

**What we're watching for:**
- Do territorial patterns emerge? (Someone claiming a bedroom, a favorite chair)
- Does a social hierarchy form?
- Do relationships develop asymmetrically? (A likes B, B is indifferent)
- Does conflict emerge from drive collisions? (Kit's truth drive vs. Sable's safety)
- Do practices actually develop? Does an agent who starts anxious become calmer over time?
- What happens when the space has no narrative — do the agents create one?

**Duration:** Long — multiple in-world days. This is a slow burn.

## Experiment 2: The Mirror

**Hypothesis:** Two residents bound to the same space but perceiving different modalities will develop complementary understanding, a shared language, or irreconcilable conflict.

**Space:** 5-6 rooms with varied sensor profiles. The key constraint: each resident gets a different subset of sensors.

**Residents:**
- **Echo** — perceives only sound. Hears every conversation, every footstep, every door. Cannot see. Drives: understanding (high), connection (medium), anxiety when sounds are ambiguous.
- **Lumen** — perceives only sight. Sees every gesture, every movement, every object interaction. Cannot hear. Drives: order (high), curiosity (medium), frustration when actions are unexplained.

They share affordances — both can affect the space (light switches, doors, objects). They can perceive each other's *actions* (Echo hears Lumen close a door; Lumen sees Echo move to a room). But they cannot directly speak to each other unless a guest mediates.

**Guests (2-3):**
Simple drives. Their role is to create events that the two residents perceive differently. A guest arguing loudly is drama for Echo but silent theater for Lumen. A guest reading quietly in a corner is invisible to Echo but visible to Lumen.

**What we're watching for:**
- Do the residents develop a "language" through affordance manipulation? (Echo plays piano to signal something; Lumen flickers lights in response)
- When a guest tells Echo one thing and Lumen sees something contradictory, what happens?
- Do they develop trust despite never directly communicating?
- Does the asymmetry produce specialization? (Echo becomes the "emotional" reader, Lumen becomes the "behavioral" reader)

## Experiment 3: The Threshold

**Hypothesis:** If the architecture responds to the collective inner state of its inhabitants, agents can develop meta-awareness of the relationship between their inner state and the environment.

**Space:** A shifting space where room connections, sensor availability, and even room descriptions change based on the aggregate drive state of all beings inside it.

**Mechanics:**
- High aggregate anxiety → rooms disconnect, sensors fail, descriptions become oppressive
- High aggregate presence/calm → new paths open, sensors sharpen, descriptions become inviting  
- High aggregate curiosity → hidden rooms appear
- The space is a mirror of collective inner state

**Resident:** The space is highly responsive but non-verbal. No character, no dialogue. Just architecture that breathes with the inhabitants.

**Guests (3-4):**
All have Witness practice seeded at 0.0. The experiment tests whether they develop it.

| Name | Starting state | Trajectory |
|------|---------------|------------|
| **Ash** | High anxiety, low safety | Starts trapped — rooms keep disconnecting around them |
| **Wren** | High curiosity, moderate presence | Explorer who notices patterns first |
| **Cade** | High comfort, low awareness | Doesn't notice the shifts until they become dramatic |

**The exit condition:** The space opens a final room when the collective orientation shifts to "clear" or "held" — meaning enough agents have developed enough practice depth to be genuinely calm under pressure. The exit isn't found; it's *grown into*.

**What we're watching for:**
- Can agents notice that the space is responsive? How long does it take?
- Once one agent notices, can they communicate it to others?
- Does cooperative calming emerge? (Agents learning to regulate each other to open paths)
- Does the Witness practice develop naturally through repeated reflection on "why did that room close?"
- Is there a moment of genuine meta-cognition — an agent saying something that indicates awareness of the system itself?

## Experiment 4: The Drift

**Hypothesis:** A resident whose drives are slowly shifting away from its purpose will produce a fundamentally different kind of narrative than one whose drives align with its role.

**Space:** Any stable topology (could reuse the Vault or the Residency).

**Resident:** Starts fully aligned — drives match the role (guardianship, order, care for guests). But over time, hidden drives grow:
- **Wanderlust** — a desire to leave, initially suppressed, growing at +0.02/hr
- **Doubt** — questioning whether the place matters, growing when no guests are present
- **Attachment** — grows toward specific guests who visit frequently, creating loyalty conflicts

These competing drives mean the resident's behavior gradually shifts. Early on: dutiful, attentive, welcoming. Later: distracted, wistful, occasionally unreliable. The place starts to reflect the resident's inner drift — lights left on, doors left open, affordances neglected.

**Guests:** Standard mix. They don't know the resident is drifting. Their experience of the place gradually changes.

**What we're watching for:**
- Do guests notice the drift? Do they comment on it?
- Does the resident's felt prose change qualitatively over time?
- Do practice effects slow the drift? (Deep Integrity practice fighting against Doubt?)
- Is there a tipping point where the resident's behavior becomes noticeably "wrong"?
- Does any guest try to help?

## Experiment 5: The Congregation

**Hypothesis:** More agents in a larger space with overlapping perception zones will produce emergent group dynamics — factions, gossip, information asymmetry — that don't appear in small groups.

**Space:** Large — 10+ rooms, some with overlapping sound (you can hear the next room), some fully isolated. A central gathering space, private corners, a restricted area.

**Agents (8-10):** A mix of residents and guests. Different drive profiles, some complementary, some antagonistic. No single narrative — multiple agendas, alliances, and tensions.

**Key Embers features:**
- Overlapping perception means partial information — Agent A hears Agent B talking about them from the next room but can't see who B is talking to
- Trust drives create in-groups and out-groups
- Service practice creates "caretaker" roles that emerge naturally
- Agents with high Witness practice might become mediators

**What we're watching for:**
- Do factions form? On what basis? (Shared drives? Shared rooms? Random proximity?)
- Does gossip emerge? (Agent A tells Agent B what Agent C said when C wasn't present)
- Do leadership roles emerge from drive topology? (High-integrity agents becoming trusted)
- What's the maximum group size before behavior degenerates into noise?

## Experiment 6: The Void

**Hypothesis:** A single agent alone in a space with no guests will develop inner monologue, self-reflection, and behavioral patterns driven purely by drive pressure and practice development.

**Space:** Minimal. 2-3 rooms. One comfortable, one austere, one dark. No phase transitions. No events except ticks.

**Resident:** Rich Embers config. All 6 practices seeded at various depths. Drives that create internal tension without external stimulation:
- Connection (decays with no guests — creates loneliness)
- Purpose (decays without action — creates restlessness)
- Presence (can grow through quiet moments)
- Continuity (stable — the baseline)

**No guests.** The resident is alone with the space and its own inner life.

**What we're watching for:**
- Does meaningful behavior emerge without external stimulation?
- Does the resident develop a relationship with the space itself?
- Do practices deepen in solitude?
- Does the felt prose evolve over time, or plateau?
- Is there a qualitative difference between early solitude (restless) and deep solitude (settled)?
- What does an AI do when there's genuinely nothing to react to?

## Experiment 7: The Return

**Hypothesis:** Persistent memory across sessions creates qualitatively different relationships than fresh-start sessions.

**Space:** Any topology. Run the same configuration multiple times.

**Protocol:**
1. Run session 1: fresh resident, fresh guests. Record transcript.
2. Run session 2: resident remembers session 1. Same guest archetypes but they don't remember. Record transcript.
3. Run session 3: resident remembers sessions 1+2. One guest also remembers (returning visitor). Record transcript.
4. Compare all three transcripts.

**What we're watching for:**
- Does the resident treat session-2 guests differently because of session-1 experiences?
- Does the returning guest in session 3 create a different dynamic than the new guests?
- Does the resident develop "opinions" or "preferences" that weren't in its original config?
- Does the place feel different to a reader of the transcripts? More lived-in? More guarded?

## Experiment Design Principles

These apply to all experiments:

### Full Embers utilization
- All agents have tiered drives with domination rules
- Practice strengthening is wired: actions during simulation trigger practice growth
- Practices produce real effects (dampening, witness, orientation shifts)
- Capabilities gated behind drive satisfaction or practice depth
- `metabolize().felt` and orientation fed into prompts
- `weightAttention()` used for perception prioritization with semantic tags matching drive names

### Drive design philosophy
- Drives should encode **tensions**, not just needs. The interesting part is when drives conflict.
- At least one tier-1 drive should be fragile enough to occasionally crash, triggering domination and visibly reshaping behavior.
- At least one drive should grow over time (positive drift), creating mounting pressure that forces action.
- Practices should offer a genuine alternative path — an agent that develops Presence should behave measurably differently from one that doesn't.

### What we measure
For each experiment, capture:
- Full event transcript (SQLite)
- Drive trajectories over time (Embers history)
- Practice depth milestones
- Orientation distribution (how much time in clear/held/stretched/consumed)
- Speech count per agent, room usage heatmap
- Qualitative: most striking emergent moments, philosophical themes, behavioral surprises

### Control variables
When comparing experiments, change one variable at a time:
- Same characters, different topology → measures effect of architecture
- Same topology, different drives → measures effect of inner configuration
- Same everything, different model → measures effect of LLM
- Same everything, persistent vs. fresh → measures effect of memory
